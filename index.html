A Faith-Based Non-Profit Inception Package rooted in divine calling, serving the vulnerable across Africa through compassion, justice, stewardship, and empowerment.

Pilot: Nigeria Scale: Africa Faith-Based

Executive Summary

Foundational Vision and Call

The inception of "THE PEOPLE'S BANQUET" (TPB) is rooted in a profound spiritual calling, described by its founder as a "clear vision from God." This foundational statement positions the organization not merely as a social enterprise but as a divinely ordained mission, guiding its entire operational philosophy and strategic direction.

Core Biblical Principles

  • Compassion: Serving with love and mercy
  • Justice: Addressing root causes of poverty
  • Stewardship: Wise resource management
  • Empowerment: Building capacity and self-reliance

Strategic Approach

  • Pilot Program: Test and refine in Nigeria
  • Scaling Strategy: Adapt across Africa
  • Partnership Model: Local collaboration
  • Impact Measurement: Rigorous evaluation

Mission, Vision, and Core Values

Mission

To follow the example of Jesus Christ by working with the poor and oppressed to promote human transformation, seek justice, and bear witness to the good news of the Kingdom of God.

"He has shown you, O mortal, what is good. And what does the Lord require of you? To act justly and to love mercy and to walk humbly with your God." — Micah 6:8

Vision

To see a transformed Africa where every individual experiences the fullness of life that God intends—communities where justice, peace, and human dignity are held sacred.

"The thief comes only to steal and kill and destroy; I have come that they may have life, and have it to the full." — John 10:10

Core Values

Compassion
Justice
Stewardship
Empowerment

Biblical Foundation of Core Values

Compassion: Serving with Love and Mercy

Compassion moves beyond sympathy to action, reflecting God's heart for the vulnerable. It involves treating each person with dignity and creating safe spaces for healing.

"Clothe yourselves with compassion" — Colossians 3:12

Justice: Upholding Fairness

Justice addresses root causes of poverty, advocates for fair policies, and challenges unjust systems that perpetuate suffering and inequality.

"Loose the chains of injustice" — Isaiah 58:6

Stewardship: Managing Resources Wisely

Stewardship recognizes that all resources are gifts from God, requiring wise management with integrity, transparency, and accountability.

Parable of the Talents — Matthew 25:14-30

Empowerment: Building Capacity

Empowerment focuses on developing self-reliance, equipping individuals and communities with tools, skills, and confidence for transformation.

"Use your gifts to serve others" — 1 Peter 4:10
